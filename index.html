About us

The sole purpose of this initiative is to build a comunity of kpop in Nepal. Kpop has been a huge sensation and it will still be, in the future, considering how Kpop music is taking over billboard charts and Grammys. One aspect of Kpop that everyone like is the dance. Unlike traditional singers, Kpop idols are groomed to be perfect in both singing and dancing. Their fans all around the world learn the same choreography and present it in their own style which we refer to as "cover dance". The cover dances of Kpop has been one of the most searched videos on the internet; alongside Kpop funny moments and their music videos. Moreover, public Kpop dances are more preferred since they highlight the performer's confidence and the bystander's unfiltered response to foreign songs. Sometimes, people take it a little too far by dancing on provocative Kpop songs on their school hallways. However, mostly public dance covers are done on department stores, busking area, walking road(vietnam), amusement parks, parks, etc. Some of the famous kpop dance cover projects are AB Project (Artbeat Project) from Korea, the A-Code from Vietnam, the J from China and East2West from Canada.

Nepal has had its fair share of some notable Kpop dance cover groups. Some of them are Strukpop, K-hoppers, Celestial girls, Witch Hazels, 4EVER1, Crimson Yeoja etc. Most of them were able to secure positions in the Kpop World Festival which is held once a year by the Embassy of Korea. However, most of them expressed their struggle with finance. Since dance covers donot generate revenue because of copyright and filming requires a lot of resources too. Keeping such things into account, Sapphire K-Cover Project was launched. We have a team of 7 members, but soon, we will be recruiting new members through audition. The group'll be practicing the choreos and filming public dance cover on different parts of Kathmandu; further expanding the hallyu wave. We will also be participating on Kpop World Festival 2021 as the representative of Nepal. The project is completely funded by our mentors and they will be mentoring the members throughout the tenure of the project. We believe that with the power of social media and our collective efforts, we can bring Kpop fans of Nepal together and build up a community of our common interest.

About our project

Sapphire K-Cover Project is an initiative launched to revolutionalize the dance industry of Nepal breaking the stereotype and prejudices of the hallyu wave; further strengthening the Kpop fan community and giving them encouragement to keep doing the things they love irrespective of the culture or language of the music. Through public dance cover on busking areas throughout the Kathmandu, the project will be acting as a catalyst to promote K-pop and eventually build a K-pop community.
